What is a Triple-Net (NNN) Property?
In a NNN lease, the tenant — not you — pays property taxes, insurance, and maintenance. You receive a net check every month. No repairs, no vacancies to fill, no rent collection. Tenants are typically investment-grade national companies: Walgreens, Dollar General, AutoZone, O'Reilly, Starbucks, or fast-food chains. Leases run 10–20 years with built-in rent bumps.

The comparison: Today your equity in this building generates $69,777 NOI with 8 tenants and active management requirements. A NNN exchange at 5.5% generates $76,841 annually — passively, with no management duties and a creditworthy national tenant on a long-term lease.
Section 4 — Unique to This Property

Federal Opportunity Zone — The Alternative to 1031

What the OZ Designation Means for a Buyer — and Potentially for You

5638 Colfax sits within a federally designated Opportunity Zone. A buyer deploying existing capital gains into an OZ-qualifying fund that acquires this property can defer those gains and eliminate tax on new appreciation after a 10-year hold. The OZ program was made permanent under the One Big Beautiful Bill Act of 2025 — it is no longer subject to sunset.

For you as the seller: if you have existing capital gains from the sale of other assets and wish to reinvest in an Opportunity Zone fund rather than a 1031 exchange, this property's OZ designation is relevant context for your tax advisor. Unlike a 1031, an OZ investment does not require like-kind property — it can be deployed into any qualifying OZ fund.

Consult your CPA or tax attorney regarding OZ eligibility, gain deferral timelines, and qualifying reinvestment vehicles. LAAA can provide introductions to qualified OZ fund managers and tax advisors familiar with the program.
